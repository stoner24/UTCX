General Rules

Rules &
Guidelines

Rule #1: Have Fun. Rule #10: Have Fun. Everything in between is just details.

Licensing

All UTCX races are USA Cycling sanctioned. To race you need either a one-day or annual USA Cycling license.

  • One-day licenses can be purchased on BikeReg or at day-of registration.
  • Annual licenses must be purchased online via the USA Cycling website.
  • Check our social media for discount codes.
  • A rider's racing age shall match their USA Cycling racing age (age on December 31 of the following year).

Registration & Check-In

All racers must check in at registration to confirm your entry, waiver, and active USAC license.

  • If your name is not on the start list you will be pulled from the race and/or will not receive a result.
  • Pre-registration is strongly encouraged — day-of registration may be available but is not guaranteed.

Equipment

Participants may ride a cyclocross, mountain, hybrid, road, single-speed, or fixed-gear bicycle. Just make sure it's in safe and working condition.

  • No tire width restrictions — we don't care how fat your tires are.
  • All bicycles must have working hand-operated brakes.
  • No bar-ends on mountain bikes, hybrids, or any bikes with flat/riser bars.
  • No aero-bars or forward handlebar extensions on drop-bar bikes.
  • No headphones or earphone devices may be worn during a race.
  • All riders must wear a helmet at all times while on the bike.

Conduct & Sportsmanship

UTCX strives to be a family-friendly event. Races are held in public parks and venues with spectators, other park users, and children who may not understand cycling culture.

  • Please treat all venue users, volunteers, officials, and fellow racers with respect.
  • Consumption of alcoholic beverages and illegal substances is prohibited in public parks in Utah.
  • Public urination is prohibited.
  • Rule interpretations and changes are at the Race Official and/or Promoter's sole discretion.
  • Be a nice person.

Rider Safety

If you come across a rider in obvious distress due to injuries or who is physically unable to continue:

  • Stop and assess the situation.
  • Render assistance if necessary.
  • Notify event staff or volunteers at the start/finish area if you can safely leave the rider.

Racing Categories

UTCX offers categories for all ages and ability levels:

  • Elite / Cat A — experienced racers competing for top honors
  • Cat B — intermediate racers with solid CX or road/MTB experience
  • Cat C — newer racers, beginners, or those racing for fitness and fun
  • Masters 35+, 45+, 55+ — age-based categories, open ability
  • Women A / B / C — women's fields across all ability levels
  • Juniors — youth categories by age group

State Championship Eligibility

The State Championship is open to all riders in the Utah Cycling Association, including riders from southeastern Idaho and western Wyoming per UCA rules (Chapter 7J3(e)). Any questions regarding residency will be determined by USA Cycling. The full category list and race-day schedule will be the same as all other UTCX races.

Questions about rules or categories? Contact us or reach out on Facebook.
